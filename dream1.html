• E–Numbers nov 3



  • 1. Escape
    Chris Morris tells you how it is by pointing towards Jean Baudrillards “There cannot be a real catastrophe because we live under the signs of virtual catastrophe.”
  • 2. Control
    The lyrics, “ambience and control/got nowhere to” derives from city planning and the term soft exclusion. How to get rid of unwanted people just by making them feel unwanted.
  • 3. Amnesia
    The sheeps recorded on a countryside farm are probably all dead, reminding us of the ever ongoing animal mass-slaughter. Also a wink to "Sheep" by The Housemartins and of course The KLF sacrificing a sheep leaving the message “I died for ewe – bon appetit”.
  • 4. Renegades
    Life is often grim and dull and the world is just a harsh place. But we strive in this, going nowhere, related and blessed, singing - Three cheers for us!
  • 5. Violence
    Jamming away in the darkest Gothenburg winter while reading Sun and Steel by Yukio Mishima and listening to slavic requiems.
  • 6. Not Here
    A feedback dream about taking detours and to lose yourself. We like situations that’s uncomfortable and eerie and it’s our subconscious attraction to awkwardness that brought us together.
  • 7. Frantic
    A homage to those who take the fight and keep questioning human life. There is only one true thing in life: passion, and it can only be expressed through murder - of others or of oneself.
  • 8. Dream On
    A buoyant-decadent house-anthem that gave birth to our new label. Tended to induce reverie, mental drift and dream on.
